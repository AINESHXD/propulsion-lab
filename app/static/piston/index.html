What you are looking at

These are ideal, air-standard numbers. The working fluid is a fixed mass of air as an ideal gas with constant specific heats; compression and expansion are isentropic; combustion is replaced by heat addition. A real engine of the same size makes noticeably less — friction, pumping work, heat loss to the walls, finite burn time and incomplete combustion all subtract. Treat efficiency and power here as the thermodynamic ceiling, not a dyno reading.
